## What we are building

Two faces on one data store. (1) An operator pipeline for Chris: every lead and account on one board, lead → contacted → onboarding → active → at_risk → churned, with revenue, source attribution, and a next action per account. (2) A customer results portal grown from the magic-link scoreboard: review growth since joining, the request → click → review funnel, usage meters. The CRM grows out of the WER Leads sheet and /admin. It does not replace them.

## Decisions locked

- Storage stays Google Sheets; operator UI extends /admin, not a separate app; overall acceptance test = a working pipeline view where stage moves persist across reloads (all decided 2026-07-12, recorded in HANDOFF.md).
- Scope answers (Chris, 2026-07-12): Stripe webhook auto-moves stages and the board shows MRR. Portal v1 is the scoreboard evolution; report archive and competitor tracking wait. Both engine steals fold in as a phase (review-detection exit rule + 180-day re-enrollment cooldown). Tasks are one next-action per account with a Today list, nothing deeper.

## Design principles, carried over and extended

The feature spec's five rules apply unchanged (one purpose, no login, ten-second comprehension, done-for-you defaults, flat price). Three CRM-specific additions: Chris is a user too, so /admin gets the same ten-second standard; the two email pools never blur (marketing drip to OUR leads is consent-gated, sequence mail to a business's customers is service traffic, and no CRM feature may mix them); every entity keeps a 1:1 mapping to a future database table so the eventual Sheets exit is mechanical.

## Stage model

Seven stages. Six are the decided taxonomy; `lost` is added here so dead leads have somewhere to go instead of clogging `contacted` (flagging it since it was not in the original list).

| Stage | Means | Enters by |
|---|---|---|
| lead | row exists (tool capture, signup, contact form) | automatic (today's behavior) |
| contacted | a human touched it | manual |
| onboarding | paid, engine not yet running | automatic on Stripe checkout completion; manual for pilots |
| active | sequences actually sending | manual, on purpose (it asserts onboarding work happened) |
| at_risk | payment failed or health flag | automatic on payment failure; manual otherwise |
| churned | subscription ended | automatic on Stripe cancellation; manual for pilots |
| lost | never paid, gone cold | manual |

One refinement to the "paid = active" answer: payment lands the account in `onboarding` automatically, and `active` stays a manual move, because between the card and the first send there is real work (list import, Place ID, templates). Cancellation → churned and payment failure → at_risk automate exactly as answered. at_risk can move back to active.

## Data model changes (all Sheets, tabs map 1:1 to future tables)

- **leads (existing tab):** add `stage`, `stage_at`, `next_action`, `next_due`, `notes`, `business_id` (links to the businesses row once onboarded).
- **businesses (existing):** add `joined_at`, `baseline_rating`, `baseline_count` (review-history backfill at onboarding, the GatherUp steal), `stripe_customer_id`, `stripe_sub_id`, `plan`, `mrr` (249 monthly, 174 annual; the intro coupon only discounts invoice one so MRR is the real rate from day one), `cooldown_days` (default 180).
- **contacts (existing):** add `last_enrolled_at` (cooldown) and `reviewed_at` (exit rule). The `consent` column is already specced by Event Mode.
- **sends (NEW tab):** ts, business, contact, campaign (sequence | reactivation | digest | drip), channel, message_id, status (sent → delivered | bounced | complained). Written at send time by tick and drip, updated by Resend webhooks. This answers "did Sarah get the email?" and the campaign column future-proofs reactivation stats.
- **suppressions (NEW tab):** business (or * for global), identifier (email or cell), reason (stop | unsubscribe | bounce | complaint | manual), ts, source. STOP and unsubscribe state migrates in; every send path checks it first.
- **events (existing):** gains `stage_move` and `review_matched` event types, so the board has an audit trail.
- **monthly_usage:** stays computed from sends at current scale; becomes a tab only if counting slows the tick.

### Phase 4: review detection: exit rule, alerts, cooldown (~1 to 1.5 days)

Rides the existing daily snapshot job. Fetch the business's newest reviews (Places returns the 5 most recent), diff against last seen, then three behaviors. Exit rule (the top NiceJob steal): fuzzy first-name match against contacts currently in sequence, only counting reviews dated after enrollment; a match sets `reviewed_at`, stops remaining sends, and logs `review_matched`; no match does nothing. Alerts: a new review at 3 stars or less immediately emails/texts Chris and the owner with a guardrailed draft from the existing /api/generate-response; 4 and up roll into the Monday digest. Cooldown: permanent dedupe-by-cell becomes a re-enrollment window, so /api/hook, kiosk, and admin add re-enroll a contact whose `last_enrolled_at` is older than `cooldown_days` (180 default) and ignore anyone fresher.
Known limit, recorded now: the 5-review window means a burst can outrun detection. Fine at current volume; GBP OAuth on the build queue is the complete fix.
**Acceptance:** a new review matching a sequenced contact's first name stops their remaining messages, and a 3-star-or-less review produces an owner alert containing a draft reply.

### Phase 5: portal v1, the scoreboard evolution (~1 day)

Same magic link, same single page, four additions: a since-joining review growth chart from the snapshots tab (baseline columns anchor the left edge), a per-step funnel (sent → clicked → reviewed per touch, from sends + review_matched), usage meters (sent vs the monthly caps, the GatherUp honesty steal), and empty-state coaching lines where a new business would otherwise see blanks. The per-source attribution line (kiosk vs email vs SMS) lands here if Event Mode has shipped by then, otherwise it ships with Event Mode.
**Acceptance:** Encore's scoreboard link shows a growth chart built from real snapshot history and a per-step funnel with real counts, behind the existing magic link, no login anywhere.

### Phase 6: monthly report + archive (~1 day)

First-of-month cron: each active business gets a plain-English email framed as growth since joining (the GatherUp Success Report framing): rating and count vs baseline, requests/clicks/reviews for the month, what we replied to. Each report is stored and the portal grows a small archive list.
**Ride-alongs (added 2026-07-13, Chris approved; ~half day on top):** (1) email-opens tracking: subscribe Resend's email.opened event on the existing webhook, opened status on sends rows, opened step in the portal funnel; (2) reviews tab `replied_at` + admin mark-replied action so digest and report can state reply coverage (Phase 7 automates the stamp); (3) industry benchmark line, hand-set averages per industry in a small lookup, one honest sentence in report and portal; (4) minimal competitor tracking (HighLevel steal, replaces "waits for competitor tracking"): businesses gains `competitor_place_ids` (up to 3, comma-separated), the daily 17 UTC snapshot block also snapshots those ids (kind=competitor rows in snapshots), and the report + portal gain a "you vs them since you joined" rating/count delta line. Places-only, no sentiment, no extra API surface.
**Acceptance:** on the 1st, every active business receives the report email and its portal lists the archived copy; for a business with a competitor_place_id set, the report shows the competitor delta line from real snapshot rows.

### Phase 7: GBP OAuth, full reviews + reply posting (queued on Google's approval, expected ~Jul 19-22)

Gated on the Business Profile API application (submitted 2026-07-12; approval = quota flips 0 to 300 QPM). Scope as already agreed: OAuth consent + refresh token with the GBP owner account, replace the Places 5-review window with full review reads (retiring Phase 4's known limit; placeReviews and the _reviews.js drafting path are the seams), post replies from the CRM, and automate `replied_at`.
**Reply behaviors baked in from the start (HighLevel steals, added 2026-07-13):** a per-business reply delay (default ~30-60 min, businesses column) so auto-replies never look instant; a backlog drip mode at onboarding that clears pre-existing unreplied reviews a few per day instead of carpet-bombing day one; approval routing where 4-5 star replies may auto-post once a business opts in and 3 stars or less ALWAYS waits for human approval through the existing alert-with-draft flow. Auto-posting defaults OFF per business until Chris flips it.
**Acceptance:** with auto-post enabled on a test business, a new 4+ star review gets an AI reply posted after the configured delay and replied_at stamps automatically; a low-star review posts nothing until the draft is approved from the alert.

## Not building (scope guard)

No separate app or domain. No customer logins or passwords, ever; magic links stay. No campaign-builder UI; sequences live in code. No per-feature upsell ladder. No gating in any form, including the portal. No mixing the marketing pool with the service pool. No database migration yet: Sheets holds until quotas or scan times actually hurt, and the 1:1 tab-to-table mapping keeps that move cheap when it comes.

## Risks recorded up front

Sheets API quotas argue for batched writes and one read per board render; trivial at current scale, worth watching past ~50 businesses. Fuzzy first-name matching can hit the wrong Mike: requiring review-time-after-enrollment, preferring no-match over wrong-match, and logging every match as an event keeps mistakes rare and auditable. The Places 5-review window can miss bursts (noted in Phase 4). The Stripe test-to-live swap touches envs and webhook secrets; the existing go-live checklist covers it. Every new admin endpoint stays behind WER_ADMIN_SECRET.

## Parked, not blocking

Stripe live activation remains Chris's item and blocks nothing here; every Stripe behavior verifies in test mode first.

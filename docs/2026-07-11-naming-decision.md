# Review Platform — Naming & Domain Research

**Date:** 2026-07-11. Companion to 2026-07-11-PLAN.md.

## Strategy (research-backed)

1. **No keyword/EMD domain.** No direct ranking benefit in 2025-26; value is CTR/brand-mediated only. Tool-query SERPs are won by descriptive page slugs on branded domains (Whitespark's google-review-link-generator page is the model), not by exact-match domains.
2. **No "google" in the name, ever.** Google Brand Resource Center prohibits incorporating their trademarks into any brand name, business name, or domain.
3. **Unique brandable = AI-citation asset.** Branded entities are cited ~6.5x more by AI assistants; brand search volume is the strongest single predictor of citations. A unique string becomes a disambiguated entity; generic names dilute.
4. **SEO plan:** brand domain + keyword tool pages (/google-review-link-generator, /google-review-qr-code, review-request templates) + removal guides ($8-12 CPC terms per the qualification doc).

## FINAL DECISION (2026-07-11): WellEarnedReviews — wellearnedreviews.com

Chosen by Chris from round 2. Registered manually in Cloudflare same day (API registration blocked: account holds 138 domains, Registrar caps new registrations at 100/account). Zone active on the Cloudflare account, id 8ce50823c5b93d735ae97ae79b9661da.

Why it won: works in all three exposures at once — consumer-facing review links read as honest ("wellearnedreviews.com/r/..." vs a "five star" domain that telegraphs rating-farming), the name carries the no-gating compliance story into every pitch, and it's a unique entity string containing "reviews" for AI citation + topical relevance.

Earlier recommendation for reference: FiveStarHabit (fivestarhabit.com); runner-up ReviewKiln (reviewkiln.com). Both left unregistered.

## Killed on collision (do not use)

- FiveStarLoop / 5starloop: **Starloop.com** is an active get-more-reviews software company.
- FiveStarNudge: **StarNudge.app** is an active five-star review product.
- Adjacent crowd noted: HiFiveStar, Fivestars (loyalty), Five Star Review System. "Five star" prefix is generic enough to coexist; coined-mark similarity is the real risk.

~70 domains checked total; review* .com namespace is heavily squatted.

## Registration notes

Brand name need not match legal entity: DBA under existing entity is the cheap path. Run a USPTO TESS knockout search on the final name before filing or spending on brand assets. (Not legal advice.)

10 situations, no two answered the same way

Negative review response examples

A good response to a negative review does four things. Thanks the customer for the specifics. Owns whatever part was actually your fault. States the fix in one sentence. Moves the rest to a phone call. It doesn't argue in public, and it doesn't put a refund number where strangers can read it either.

Below are 10 real situations with a reply you can copy and adjust. Or skip the copying entirely: paste your review into the generator and let it write the first draft. If you'd rather fix the review side before you worry about replies, the review request templates are free too, and they take less time than reading this whole page.

Late arrival, no phone call

This is the shape a negative review takes most often: something slipped, and nobody called to say so.

REVIEW: "Guy showed up two hours late and never called."You're right, and I'm sorry. We should've called the second we knew we were running behind, that's on us and not on you. I've already talked to the crew about giving a heads-up the moment a job runs long. Call the shop, ask for me directly, and I'll make the next visit right.

It names the actual failure, no call, instead of hiding behind a vague apology. The fix is procedural, not a bribe.

Price dispute

REVIEW: "Way overpriced compared to the guy down the street."I hear you, and no, we're not the cheapest quote in town. We price for licensed, insured work and parts with a real warranty behind them. That's worth knowing if the cheaper option doesn't hold up. Happy to walk through what was included, just give the shop a call.

No apology for the price. One calm explanation, once, then the door open to keep talking off the page.

Something went wrong, and someone was there for it

REVIEW: "The front desk person was short with me and made me feel dumb for asking questions."That's not how we want anyone to feel walking out of here, and I'm sorry it happened. I'd like to know exactly what was said so I can address it with the team, not to argue, just to fix it. Please call and ask for the manager on duty.
REVIEW: "Ordered the medium-rare ribeye, got well done, and the table next to us got it right."That should never leave the kitchen like that. Full stop. I've flagged it with the line so it doesn't happen again. Next time you're in, ask for me by name. Dinner's on the house.

Neither reply confirms details nobody outside the building can verify, and neither one argues. The ribeye fix happens face to face too, not as a discount code someone could screenshot and pass around.

No-show accusation

REVIEW: "Booked a 2pm appointment and nobody ever showed. Waste of my afternoon."I looked back through our schedule and don't see a booking under this name for that date. That doesn't mean something didn't fall through the cracks on our end. Could you call the shop with whatever confirmation you got, text or email, so I can trace what happened? I want to get this right either way.

A negative review that says nothing

REVIEW: [1 star, no comment]We'd genuinely like to know what went wrong here. Call the shop or reply with a few details and we'll do what we can to make it right.

Suspected fake review

REVIEW: "Terrible company, avoid at all costs" (no order history, no name match, posted the same week a competitor opened nearby)We couldn't find any record of this visit under the name or dates listed. If we've got something wrong, reach out directly so we can sort it out. If it was left in error, we'd appreciate it being removed.

States a fact, no matching record, instead of leveling an accusation back. Reads as professional either way, fake review or not.

The refund ask, and the one that got forgotten

REVIEW: "Half the job was fine but the trim work was sloppy. Want some money back."Fair callout on the trim. That part wasn't up to our standard, and I'd rather fix it than argue about it. Call the shop and we'll get someone back out to redo it, no charge.
REVIEW: "Never came back to fix what they promised back in March, still waiting."You're right to be frustrated, and I'm sorry this fell off our radar. Call the shop and reference this review so we can find your job and get someone scheduled this week.

Redoing the work beats negotiating a number where anyone can read it. And a timeframe (this week) means more to a stranger than any excuse for the delay ever could.

Genuine misunderstanding

REVIEW: "Thought the estimate included the permit fee, it didn't, felt misled."I can see how that would be confusing. Permit fees are usually a separate line, and we should've called that out more clearly on the estimate. We're updating our quote template so it's spelled out for everyone going forward. Sorry for the confusion this time.

This is the one where nobody did anything wrong on purpose, and the reply treats the customer like a reasonable person instead of someone who skipped the fine print. The fix, a clearer quote template, is a process change, not just an apology tacked onto the end of a sentence. That matters because the review sits there for years. A one-line sorry ages badly. A named fix doesn't.

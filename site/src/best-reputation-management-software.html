Seven tools, one honest question

Best reputation management software for local businesses

Full disclosure before a single ranking: this site belongs to WellEarnedReviews, and we're one of the seven options below. Read accordingly. We think the reasoning holds up anyway, which is why we published it with our name on it.

Here's the question that actually sorts this category, and it isn't price. Who is going to log in? Reputation management software is an inbox plus automation rules, and somebody has to feed it. A multi-location brand has that somebody. Most single-location businesses do not, and a dashboard nobody opens is the most expensive kind of cheap.

Match the tool to your situation

You run multiple locations and someone on staff owns marketing. Look at Birdeye first ($299 to $449 a month per location as published, annual minimum, and buyers report cancellation only at renewal). It's built for exactly you: listings, reviews, and reporting at scale, priced like it. We wrote up what Birdeye actually costs and who its alternatives are if that price made you flinch.

You want reviews and messaging and payments in one place, and you'll operate it. That's Podium, $249 to $599 a month published. Strong messaging suite. Annual-billing complaints show up a lot in buyer reviews, so read the contract twice. If you're weighing it against Birdeye, we did that head-to-head; if you're weighing it against everything, here's the field.

You'll run it yourself and want to spend less. Two honest picks. NiceJob ($75 to $174 a month, plus a $199 setup fee on their Sites product) is the friendliest of the self-serve tools. GatherUp ($99 a month plus $60 per extra location) costs less up front and caps you at 300 texts and 3,000 emails per location monthly, which is plenty for most and worth knowing about regardless.

You hate tiers. Starloop: $197 flat, explicitly unlimited usage, no contract, no kidding. The tradeoff is a thinner feature set and, based on their organic footprint, a much smaller company behind it.

You want the cheapest possible entry. ReviewGrower starts at $37 a month with tiered SMS. It'll do the job at small volume. The interface shows the price.

The questions under the question

Do I need reputation management software at all?

If you're under roughly ten reviews a month across all platforms, honestly, maybe not. A saved review link, a habit of texting it after every job, and fifteen minutes a week replying gets you most of the value. The tools earn their keep when volume or location count makes the manual habit break down. The free version of that habit is on our templates page.

What does this actually cost per year?

More than the sticker. Birdeye at three locations runs $10,764 to $16,164 a year at published rates. Podium's midrange lands around $4,000 to $7,000. The self-serve tools run $900 to $2,100. Then add the unlisted line item: the hours someone spends operating whichever one you pick. That number is zero exactly once on this page.

Why is a service listed in a software roundup?

Because the person searching this phrase usually doesn't want software. They want the outcome the software promises, more reviews and answered reviews, and the software is just the middleman between them and some unpaid evening admin work. Listing only dashboards would answer the search term and dodge the question.

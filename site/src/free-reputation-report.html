Quick answers

Where do these numbers come from?

Straight from your public Google Business Profile listing, the same rating and review count anyone sees when they search for you. We don't estimate anything; the report is your live listing plus arithmetic.

How is "reviews needed" calculated?

Plain math on your current average. If you sit at 4.2 with 40 reviews, we calculate how many new five-star reviews it takes for the weighted average to cross 4.5 and 4.8. No model, no projection, just the formula Google's average implies.

Why does the average matter so much?

Search results show your stars before anyone reads a word. Crossing common filter thresholds like 4.5 changes how many people tap your listing instead of the next one. Recency and volume matter too, which is why steady asking beats a once-a-year push.

Is this really free?

Yes. The report is free and instant. We make our money on a paid service that does the review asking for you, and this is how we'd rather introduce ourselves.

Podium alternative: 5 options, honestly compared

Most people who type "Podium alternative" into Google have already looked at the price. Podium's published plans run $249 to $599 a month, and enough of the reviews and buyer forums out there mention annual billing that's harder to escape than expected. That's usually reason one. Reason two is quieter but matters more day to day: Podium is software. A dashboard, an inbox, a webchat widget, a phone system to configure, and somebody on the team has to actually run it. For a lot of small operations, that somebody is the owner, at 9pm, after the jobs are done.

So the search isn't really "what's cheaper than Podium." It's "what gets me more Google reviews without turning into a part-time software administrator." Below are five real options, what each one publishes as its price, and who it actually fits. Prices change and vendors run promos, so treat every number here as published-as-of-2026, not a quote.

WellEarnedReviews: the Podium alternative you don't have to operate

You don't have to run anything. That's the whole pitch, and yes, this is us, so read the rest with that in mind. NiceJob

$75 to $174 a month, depending on plan, plus a $199 setup fee if you add their website builder (Sites). That's NiceJob: self-serve review and marketing software covering review requests, some marketing automation, and a website product if you want one. You log in, you build the sequences, you watch the dashboard yourself. Fine if you like having your hands on the controls and don't mind the setup work, and it undercuts Podium at the low end. Not a fit if the whole reason you're looking is to get this off your plate.

GatherUp

Multi-location operators who want one consistent tool across sites, and don't mind running it themselves, tend to land on GatherUp. It runs $99 a month for one location, plus $60 for each additional one, and publishes usage caps of 300 SMS and 3,000 emails per location per month, numbers that track closely with what a single busy shop actually sends. It's a straightforward review request and monitoring tool built for people comfortable managing their own campaigns: still software you operate, just cheaper software than Podium.

Birdeye

Birdeye plays a different game entirely: listings management, reputation monitoring, and review requests across many locations at once, published at $299 to $449 a month per location, with an annual minimum (some buyers have reported terms as long as three years, and cancellation windows tied to renewal dates). Running five, ten, fifty locations and need one system watching all of them? That's where Birdeye's per-location pricing and feature depth start to make sense. For a single-location business, it's an expensive way to do something a much smaller tool handles fine, and the annual commitment is a real cost, not a footnote.

Starloop

$197 a month, flat, no location or usage tiers, and unlimited sends with no per-message caps to track. That's Starloop's whole pitch, aimed at owners who want one predictable number on the invoice and are fine running the send schedule themselves. Worth a look if you want self-serve software without a calculator attached. Still a tool you log into and operate, same as NiceJob and GatherUp.

Which one is actually right for you

One location, and the job is just getting more Google reviews without babysitting a platform? WellEarnedReviews is built for exactly that gap. Want a phone system, webchat, and payments bundled with review requests, and you're prepared to run it yourself? Podium's suite does more than any name here, at the price to match it. Several locations, and you need one dashboard watching all of them? Birdeye or GatherUp's per-location pricing scales in a way a single-location tool doesn't. And if the goal is just the cheapest self-serve option with one flat number, Starloop or NiceJob's lower tier gets you there. None of these are the wrong answer, they're built for different amounts of your own time. That's the whole ballgame.
